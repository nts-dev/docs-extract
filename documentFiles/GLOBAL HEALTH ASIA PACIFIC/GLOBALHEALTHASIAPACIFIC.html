1.1        Course description

Healthcare is one of the most basic and fundamental needs of human being. Across the globe, healthcare provision is unevenly distributed which can be attributed to various factors such as socio-economic level, availability of infrastructure, etc. This course, Global Health and Asia Pacific offers knowledge of various health systems and practices across the world, while introducing the systems/practices the Asia Pacific region to complement the Western-oriented knowledge.

This course will give knowledge to students on the concept of Global Health, concepts of Western health systems and broaden their perspectives other health systems in the world for examples those in Asia Pacific. This allows students to understand what the interplay of the healthcare system and stakeholders is by the comparisons between healthcare system works and policies. There will also be discussion on the applications of Global Health to give students practical knowledge and information on what they can apply on their (future) work in the healthcare field.

The Asia Pacific region, which is home close to 60% of the world’s population, is one region that consists of countries with varying level of healthcare systems sophistication and maturity (reimbursed, semi-reimbursed and out-of-pocket countries). The region has also seen substantial economic growth in the last decades allowing the healthcare system to transform and evolve in certain developing countries. Given these characteristics, the region offers interesting discussion points from the healthcare system, stakeholders and public health/ health-related policies perspectives to the Western- oriented understanding on the global health issues.

The course will include various case studies, recent health events such as the COVID-19 pandemic to illustrate how the healthcare ecosystem and its stakeholders at the local, regional and global level interact with each other. Discussions of these case studies will revolve around how the public health or health-related policies affect and compare with those in Western countries or at a larger global level. In addition, the course will also review how the Western and Asian healthcare systems can benefit/ contribute to the overall global health and health for all.

6.        Assignment format and grading criteria

6.1               In-class group presentation

As a group, students will be responsible for the moderation and content of the seminar, but individual contributions have to be indicated.

Introduction:

Students will form four (4) presentation groups for the seminars. The groups are formed base their presentation on the assigned readings.

In each of the seminars of the course, In-class group presentations are given by a different group of students. Within these presentations’ students discuss a topical issue or a specific about Global Health case (i.e. a news item, a short movie, a case found within a newspaper or documentary, trending videos seen on YouTube etc.) that relates to the literature and theme of the respective lectures.

Students prepare an interactive session, for example by organizing a debate, quiz, discussion, game,role play, etc. Students are free (and even encouraged) to think creatively of how to conduct the session.

In essence, students will not present a summary of the literature, since the literature will already be discussed during the lecture. Instead, students will connect the literature to a relevant subject of their choice. The interactive session will be given by groups of 4-5 students and have a duration of 30 to 45 minutes.

Assessment criteria:

·           Content

o   Main points of the arguments are clear; presenters get their messagesd through

o   Presentation enhance the understanding of the topic; presenters make links to the topics/ theories/ literatures discussed in the course

o   Students present the topic in a clear, interesting, original and/or creative way, for example, students provide clear and original propositions

·           Structure and set-up

o   Presentation is clearly structured and consistent

o   All group members cooperate and substantially contribute to the presentation

o   Presenters encourage the whole seminar group to actively participate

 

6.2               Written assignment

Introduction:

The report should be well-structured, written accurately and clearly. Students may choose any topics of their interests that are discussed in the lectures and/or seminars on Global Health globally or in different regions/ countries.

Students can seek advices on their proposals (topic, key issues/ problem statement, information used, overall plan for the report) from the course instructors in the last seminar. Feedback on the proposals will be provided to the students in the seminar.

Conditions:

·           The essay is a group assignment (in principle 3 students in a group). The assignment cannot be done alone and a group cannot be larger than 3 students

·           The essay should be written in English and formatted in APA-style (for in-text referencing and the bibliography/ reference list)

·           The essay should not contain more than 2500 words (excluding title page, table of contents and bibliography)

The report should contain the following aspects:

·           Title page

Course name, course code, course year, title of the report, name of the students, word count of the main text (excluding title page, table of contents and bibliography)

·           An introduction on the topic chosen

Brief background information on the chosen topic and its relevance to Global Health and/or its application at national or international level. Explanation on why the topic has been chosen.

·           Key issues or problem statements

Outline of key issues or problems related to the chosen topic. For example, the chosen topic is healthcare systems in the developed and developing countries. Key issues/ problem statements for this topic could be: How are the healthcare systems in developed and developing countries different from each other? What are the key challenges faced by the healthcare ecosystems? Why the topics and key issues are important to address?

·           Discussion on the key issues/ problems and conclusion

Thorough discussion on the information to address the key issues/ problems. For example, what are the public discourse about this issue or problem? What are the policies or research about it? What are the future trends to tackle this global health issue in these countries? What is your own recommendation?

·           Bibliography/ reference list.

Correctly and accurately use the APA referencing style for in-text referencing as well as the reference list. Consult APA Publication Manual (6th edition) for all details.

Possible sources of information:

·           Scientific literature, such as articles, books, readings or reviews

·           Policy documents

·           Literatures cited in this course

·           News sources
